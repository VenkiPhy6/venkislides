How to reason about the estimand

  • Step 1: Draw that table as it applies for the operationalization in the paper you are reading.
  • Step 2: Think about in which rows we are filling in the unobserved potential outcomes (i.e) Who’s counterfactual are we actually proxying?

Note:

  • Generally, Matching only fills $Y^0_i$ of treated units’ rows. That is why it is ATT.
  • Generally, DiD only fills $Y^0_i$ of treated units’ rows. That is why it is ATT.

Back to the question - e) What is the estimand the authors use?

Note that the treatment is in dosages (i.e) a ratio between 0 and 1.

  • That amounts to admitting everyone has some treatment. So all are somewhat treated which means ATT (what DiD gives) $\equiv$ ATE
  • But then effects are weighted by the dosage. So it is weighted ATE.

f) What assumptions are made by the identification strategy? …

  1. Conditional Parallel Trends Assumption (PTA): Conditionally on included controls, the pass rate in different districts would develop the same way if they had the same regime.
  2. Backdoor is closed: The schooling mode imposed on each district is not dependent on the pass rate of the students in the district.

The treatment is conditional one only one parent - COVID which is an exogenous shock.

Including State-year, CZ-year and county-year makes them unidentifiable! It is not a mere multicollinearity.

Some metacognition

See that all questions fit into 4 main categories:

  1. Hypothesis questions: How can I narrate this problem and its direction without looking at any data?
  2. Sampling issues: Now that I understand the problem what type of data to ideally get? What do we actually get?
  3. Methodological issues: With the collected data, what assumptions to satisfy to isolate hypothesized causal effect? What method to use that satisfies these assumptions?
  4. Interpreting the result

Did any of that require ChatGPT? No!

When you are stuck answering these questions ask the following:

  1. Is it a hypothesis question?
    • Then, just understand the world the authors are narrating. What is their story?
